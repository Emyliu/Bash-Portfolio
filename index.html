SickKids Research Institute


During Fall 2019, I worked at the Peter Gilgan Center for Research and Learning in Toronto, which is the research facility associated with The Hospital for Sick Children

I worked on full-stack development tasks for an initiative called CHILDdb, which is the largest longitudinal child health study ever done in Canada

I developed various features for the CHILDdb web portal, such as an Amazon S3-based storage solution, database development, and a email-based password reset system

Overall, it was a great experience and I worked with some interesting technologies like GraphQL and pure functional JS using a library called Ramda

IBM


During Winter 2019, I worked at the IBM Software Lab in Markham, Ontario. I was a developer supporting Technical Account Managers (TAM), which focus on helping IBM clients worldwide

In this role, I worked on full-stack development on various different toolsets in use, as the TAMs used different pieces of software spread in a microservice architecture

With another intern, we created an extension to the microservices called Client-Notes, which lets TAMs more accurately track their customer engagements

I also architected a single-sign-on and preferences saver that connected all the existing microservices together

There was a lot to do every day at IBM. It was an amazing learning experience and definitely helped me get used to working with large codebases
